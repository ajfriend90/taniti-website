Frequently Asked Questions

Home > FAQs
What type of power outlets does Taniti use?

Taniti uses 120-volt electrical outlets, the same as those used in the United States. No voltage converter is needed if you're traveling from the U.S.

What are the rules for purchasing alcohol?

Alcohol cannot be sold or served between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The legal drinking age is 18, though enforcement may be more relaxed than in some countries.

Is English spoken widely on the island?

Yes, many younger Tanitians speak fluent English, especially in cities and tourist areas. In rural regions, however, English is less commonly spoken.

What kind of medical care is available?

Taniti has one hospital and several clinics. Many of the hospital staff speak multiple languages, and visitors can expect quality basic care for common issues.

Is Taniti a safe place to visit?

Violent crime is extremely rare. Visitors should still use common sense and be aware of petty crimes like pickpocketing, especially in busy tourist spots.

Will businesses be open on holidays?

Taniti celebrates many national holidays, and restaurants or attractions may be closed during these times. Plan ahead to avoid disruptions to your itinerary.

What currency is accepted on the island?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar. Many businesses also accept euros and yen, and currency exchange is available at several banks. Major credit cards are widely accepted.

How can I get around once I'm on the island?

Taniti has reliable public buses in the city, private buses around the island, as well as taxis, bike rentals, and a local car rental agency near the airport.

Do I need a visa to visit Taniti?

Most short-term visitors do not need a visa, but it's best to check current travel requirements based on your country of origin before booking.

Can I use my cell phone on the island?

Most international phone plans work on Taniti, especially in urban areas. Check with your provider about roaming charges or consider using local Wi-Fi.

What is the best time of year to visit Taniti?

The island enjoys a tropical climate year-round. The most popular times to visit are during the dry season, which typically runs from May to October.
